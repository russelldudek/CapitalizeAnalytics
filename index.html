The operating contradiction

That is not two jobs awkwardly combined. It is the role’s source of leverage. The consultant who preserves context across the handoffs can reduce rework, improve estimates, strengthen trust, and leave behind reusable practice knowledge.

The operating model

The AI Proof Ledger keeps one engagement from becoming six disconnected conversations.

Each record answers a decision the Senior Consultant should make easier. The ledger is not paperwork. It is the mechanism that preserves assumptions, authority, evidence, and ownership while the work moves.

CONTINUITY RECORD
RecordDecision it clarifiesFailure it prevents
01 · Decision record

What outcome, workflow, consequence, and owner define the real problem?

Prevents technology-first scoping.
02 · Leverage hypothesis

How should AI change speed, economics, quality, or risk—and what evidence could disprove the bet?

Prevents vague ROI language.
03 · Authority boundary

Which actions may the system take, and where must a person decide, review, override, or stop?

Prevents accountability drift.
04 · Evidence standard

Which representative cases, quality thresholds, business measures, and failure modes determine readiness?

Prevents demo success from masquerading as production readiness.
05 · Handoff contract

What integrations, permissions, observability, support, rollback, and training obligations must be explicit?

Prevents the proof from dying in transition.
06 · Ownership cadence

Who reviews performance, exceptions, user behavior, risk, and improvement after delivery?

Prevents consultant dependency.

What the role should make measurable

A proof is only useful when it changes a decision.

Targets should be set with practice leadership after baseline discovery. These are the categories I would instrument before calling an engagement—or the practice—successful.

Commercial

Ideation-to-paid-engagement conversion, proposal cycle time, estimate accuracy, expansion path.

Delivery

Time to first working evidence, scope change, rework, evaluation pass rate, handoff quality.

Client value

Use-case-specific cycle time, quality, cost, risk, throughput, or revenue measure.

Trust

Groundedness, override patterns, escalation quality, permission failures, auditability, incidents.

Adoption

Use in the target workflow, abandonment points, training, confidence, owner review cadence.

Practice leverage

Reusable discovery assets, evaluation sets, reference patterns, demos, and practitioner enablement.

First 90 days

Earn context. Co-deliver proof. Then compound learning.

This is a hypothesis for discovery, not a pre-baked prescription. The sequence protects client delivery while revealing where the practice repeatedly loses time, trust, or reusable knowledge.

01–30

Read the system

Shadow ideation, demos, scoping, delivery, handoff, and retrospectives. Map decision rights, reference patterns, and repeated friction.

OutputPractice friction register, decision-rights map, and evidence baseline.
31–60

Co-deliver the proof

Co-lead one opportunity into a decision brief, representative evaluation set, governed prototype, and explicit production-handoff checklist.

OutputTested engagement canvas, evaluation rubric, and handoff pattern.
61–90

Lead and teach

Lead a bounded workstream, run the retrospective, and convert validated learning into one reusable asset, one enablement session, and one stop condition.

OutputClient evidence, practitioner learning, and a prioritized next bet.

Red-team the hire

The strongest objection should be tested, not hidden.

The concern “Is Russell current and hands-on enough for a role that expects production-ready AI delivery across modern data platforms—not only strategy and operations leadership?”

The truth I should not claim production ownership of every platform named in the posting. My verified toolkit includes Python, SQL, TensorFlow, LLM/RAG patterns, agentic workflows, human-in-the-loop design, and direct AI-assisted operating transformation.

The bridge The role explicitly combines executive advisory, ideation, scoping, demos, solution design, and delivery. My differentiator is technical fluency plus accountability for the workflows, controls, people, and consequences AI must improve.

The proof plan Give me a practical case: frame the decision, define the authority boundary, write the evaluation plan, sketch the architecture, and pair-review implementation choices.

Executive interview questions

Questions that reveal where the role creates leverage.

  1. Where does the AI practice currently lose the most momentum between ideation, scope, proof, production handoff, and client adoption?
  2. What outcome matters most in the first six months: billable delivery, presales conversion, reusable practice IP, partner motion, or a deliberately balanced mix?
  3. How are decision rights divided when an opportunity crosses AI, data engineering, automation, account growth, and strategic alliances?
  4. What has Capitalize learned from its production AI work that should change how future ideation sessions are run or engagements are scoped?
  5. Which parts of the 45-day model are deliberately standardized, and where should a Senior Consultant exercise judgment?
  6. When a proof is technically successful but behavior does not change, who owns the adoption problem?
  7. What would make leadership say after one year, “this person made the whole practice easier to sell, deliver, and trust”?
