Operating principles and Days 1–30

Principles

Protect delivery

Learn inside real work rather than creating a parallel transformation project.

Keep assumptions visible

Use the AI Proof Ledger to preserve decision context across handoffs.

Instrument early

Define value, trust, and adoption measures before demo momentum takes over.

Earn reuse

Promote an asset only after real work proves it portable.

Days 1–30 · Read the system

Understand how opportunities become delivery commitments.

  • Shadow ideation sessions, sales demos, scoping conversations, delivery rituals, handoffs, and retrospectives.
  • Map the path from lead signal to use-case choice, estimate, build, evaluation, production handoff, adoption, and expansion.
  • Learn current reference architectures, security and governance patterns, partner motions, and escalation paths.
  • Interview practitioners and client-facing leaders about where work is repeatedly re-explained, re-scoped, or rebuilt.
  • Compare proposal assumptions with delivery reality on recent engagements.

Outputs: practice friction register; decision-rights map; baseline scorecard; one priority improvement hypothesis.

Questions to answer before changing anything

  • Where does context get lost between seller, advisor, builder, and client owner?
  • Which estimates fail because assumptions are implicit?
  • What proof criteria are defined too late?
  • Which reusable assets are actually reused—and which merely exist?

Stakeholder listening map

Practice leadership

Growth goals, portfolio mix, delivery quality, and where senior judgment is most needed.

Practitioners

Repeated rework, missing context, architecture patterns, and enablement gaps.

Client-facing leaders

Qualification, promise-to-delivery friction, objections, and expansion signals.

Client owners

Workflow reality, acceptable failure, adoption burden, and long-term ownership.

Days 31–60 and 61–90

Days 31–60 · Co-deliver the proof

Attach the operating model to real client work.

  • Co-lead one ideation or discovery sequence and convert the opportunity into a decision brief with value, data, risk, human authority, and proof criteria.
  • Build or pair-build a bounded demo or reference pattern within the sanctioned technical stack.
  • Create a representative evaluation set and document failure modes before the client demo.
  • Draft a scoping and production-handoff checklist that connects estimate assumptions to integrations, permissions, observability, support, training, and rollback.
  • Instrument value and adoption measures at the beginning of the engagement.

Outputs: tested engagement canvas; evaluation rubric; decision brief; production-handoff pattern.

Days 61–90 · Lead and teach

Turn one engagement into evidence for the next.

  • Lead a bounded engagement or major workstream end to end with appropriate leadership support.
  • Run a retrospective against estimate, proof criteria, client adoption, and reusable practice learning.
  • Convert validated learning into one enablement session, one reusable asset, and one documented stop condition.
  • Establish an ownership cadence with the client for performance, exceptions, risk, and improvement.
  • Propose the next practice improvement only after evidence shows where leverage exists.

Outputs: client evidence; practitioner learning; reusable asset; prioritized next bet.

Day-90 review

Commercial

Did the engagement become easier to qualify, estimate, and explain?

Delivery

Did the team reach credible evidence faster with less rework?

Trust

Were authority, failure modes, and escalation visible?

Ownership

Can the client operate and improve the solution without permanent dependence?
